About Me

I am a recent AI M.Sc. graduate from the computing department at Imperial College London. My research interests focus on machine learning and artificial intelligence, with a recent emphasis on object representation learning techniques in biomedical imaging segmentation. I am passionate about leveraging my expertise in data science and AI to solve complex problems across various fields, including healthcare, finance, and technology.

Background

My journey in the world of data and technology began with a B.Sc. in Actuarial and Financial Studies from University College Dublin, where I honed my mathematical skills and analytical thinking. This foundation led me to roles in finance and data science, including positions at AerPrice and Aviva, where I developed innovative tools and managed complex financial portfolios.

At Imperial College London, I deepened my knowledge in AI, tackling advanced topics such as Symbolic AI, Reinforcement Learning, Deep Learning, and Machine Learning for Imaging. My master's research focused on applying object representation learning techniques to biomedical imaging segmentation, showcasing my ability to work on cutting-edge AI applications in healthcare.
